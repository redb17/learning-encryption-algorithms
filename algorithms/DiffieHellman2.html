The Pros of Diffie Hellman:

  1. Secret key is established without any prior physical meeting.
  2. It provides Forward Secrecy if it is decided to generate new key for each session.

The Cons of Diffie Hellman:

  1. It does not provide the Authentication of the two parties. In our example of P1 and P2, an attacker P3 on the network might act as P2 for actual P1 and P1 for actual P2. P3 will establish a key k13 for himself and P1; and a another key k32 for himself and P2. Hence, all the messages can be read, changed and forwarded to P1 and P2 with P3 sitting in between.
