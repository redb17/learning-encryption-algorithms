Problems with Caesar Cipher:

  1. There has to be a prior physical meeting between the sender and receiver.
  2. An attacker can keep collecting the messages until he gets the key and then all the prior messages can be decrypted hence no forward secrecy.
  3. If an attacker gets the key then he could act as the receiver (for the actual sender) and sender (for the actual receiver); and not just read but also could change the messages between the two.
